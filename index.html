Ethiopia Research Project

Overview

As of 2025, Ethiopia has a population of 135 million. It is the 10th-most populous country in the world but faces significant economic challenges. Over 58 million people do not have access to clean water.

"What is the impact of climate variability on economic growth in Ethiopia?"

Climate & Agriculture

96% of cropland is rain-fed, making farmers extremely vulnerable to weather changes. Agriculture generated 34.5% of the total GDP in 2020.

Education

Work and school compete for children's time. Primary enrollment is around 58%, while secondary enrollment drops significantly to 2.5%.

Effect

A 1% increase in temperature variability can decrease annual GDP by up to 4.5%.

Summary

Climate adaptation must be a core part of macroeconomic planning to break the poverty cycle.
