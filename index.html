Climate Variability: The Impact on Ethiopia’s Economy & Persistent Poverty

Overview

As of 2025, Ethiopia has a population of 135 million. It is the 10th-most populous country in the world but faces significant economic challenges.

"What is the impact of climate variability on economic growth in Ethiopia?"

Climate & Agriculture

96% of cropland is rain-fed, making farmers extremely vulnerable to weather changes.

Education

Work and school compete for children's time. Higher rainfall often pulls boys out of school to help with labor.

Economy

Effect & NARDL

A 1% increase in temperature variability can decrease annual GDP by up to 4.5%.

Summary

Climate adaptation must be a core part of macroeconomic planning to break the poverty cycle.

Initiative

Financing through EIB Global and SOS Children's Villages are helping modernize farming.
